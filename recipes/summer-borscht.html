Summer borscht

Image credited to Off-Shell via the Creative Commons License 4.0.

Description

I always remember going to my grandparents house, and when dinner time came my grandma went into the pantry and came back with a jar of soup. She would heat it up filling the house with a strong dill and sorrel smell that's become very nostalgic for me. We would then sit down and eat bowls of hot summer borscht cooled down slightly with a spoon of sour cream, as well as crusty buns she had made with butter spread all over.

Ingredients

  • 2qts soup stock (boil ham bone for 1 hour)
  • 2c. finely chopped sorrel or beet leaves
  • 1/2c. chopped onion greens
  • 1/3c. dill greens
  • 3-4 medium potatoes
  • 1/2c. sweet cream
  • 1/2c. thick sour milk
  • salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Bring stock to a boil.
  2. Add sorrel/beetleaves, onion greens, dill, and potatoes and boil until potatoes are cooked.
  3. Add the cream and sour milk, and season with salt and pepper to your liking.
